Component overview

Button

Buttons are the most basic interaction element in a user interface, as they allow users to perform actions or navigate the interface. Below you have two variations of a button, one primary and one disabled.

Card

Cards are used to group information and make it easier to read. They can include images, text, buttons, and other elements. Cards are often clickable and are commonly used on websites to display a list of articles, products, or other types of content. Below you see two types of cards, one hover-focused and a flip card:

Toggle

A toggle is used to quickly switch between two possible states. They are commonly used for “on/off” switches. Below you have a toggle switch and a like toggle.

Dropdown

Dropdowns are used to allow users to choose an option from a list without displaying all the options at once. They are useful for selecting from a large list of items, such as in navigation menus, form selections, or filtering options.

Alert

Alerts are used to display important messages to the user, such as error messages or confirmations. They are commonly found in all types of applications to draw the user's attention to something important. Alerts can have different styles depending on the severity (e.g., info, warning, error).
